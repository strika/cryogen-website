There are ants on the page!

Yes there are! And not just any ants, but Langton's ants [1]. Langton's ant is a two-dimensional Turing machine with a very simple set of rules:

  • At a white square, turn 90° right, flip the color of the square, move forward one unit
  • At a black square, turn 90° left, flip the color of the square, move forward one unit

Although rules are very simple, they can produce complex behaviour. It was invented by Chris Langton [2] in 1986.

I implemented the ant for fun in JavaScript - Langton's ant. I find it to be a great "programming excercise/kata + a geeky computer history lesson" combo.
